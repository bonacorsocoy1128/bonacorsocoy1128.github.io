1960s:
1. Mod fashion: This style involved bold, geometric patterns, mini skirts, and shift dresses.
2. Hippie fashion: This style included flowy maxi dresses, bell-bottom pants, and tie-dye prints.
3. Twiggy-inspired fashion: Twiggy, a popular model of the era, popularized the „coltish” look, which included mini skirts, oversized sunglasses, and a boyish haircut.
4. Go-go boots: These knee-high boots became popular during the 1960s and were often worn with skirts or dresses.

Today:
1. Athleisure: This trend combines athleticwear with everyday fashion, with people wearing leggings, joggers, and sneakers as everyday clothing.
2. Streetwear: Streetwear fashion is influenced by urban culture and often includes baggy clothing, graphic t-shirts, hoodies, and sneakers.
3. Minimalism: Many people today opt for a more minimalistic wardrobe, with clean lines, neutral colors, and simple yet high-quality pieces.
4. Sustainability: With increasing awareness of environmental issues, many people today prioritize buying sustainable and eco-friendly fashion items. This trend often includes thrifting, upcycling, and choosing ethically produced clothing.

These are just a few examples of popular fashion trends in the 1960s and today. Fashion is constantly evolving, and there are always new trends emerging.
