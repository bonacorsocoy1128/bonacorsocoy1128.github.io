In supervised learning, the machine learning system is trained on a labeled dataset, where the input data is paired with the correct output or outcome. Unsupervised learning, on the other hand, involves training a machine learning system on unlabeled data, allowing it to discover patterns or structures in the data on its own. Semi-supervised learning is a combination of supervised and unsupervised learning, where the system is trained on a mixture of labeled and unlabeled data. Reinforcement learning involves training a system to learn from its interactions with an environment, receiving feedback in the form of rewards or punishments for its actions.
