Here are a few:

1. Online forums and communities: Join forums and communities dedicated to cybersecurity and malware analysis. Websites like Malwarebytes Forum or BleepingComputer have sections where you can ask questions and seek help from experts in the field or other users facing similar challenges.

2. Online courses and tutorials: Enroll in online courses or watch tutorials that cover topics related to malware analysis. Websites like Udemy, Coursera, or Cybrary offer courses specifically designed to help individuals learn about malware analysis, including tools and techniques used.

3. Malware analysis tools: Utilize specialized tools that assist in analyzing malware. Tools like IDA Pro, OllyDbg, or Cuckoo Sandbox can help you analyze the behavior of malware, identify network traffic, and understand how the malware operates.

4. Cybersecurity research organizations: Reach out to cybersecurity research organizations like MITRE or CERT, which provide resources and guidance on malware analysis. They often publish reports and provide technical guidance on how to analyze and counter specific types of malware.

5. Penetration testing and cybersecurity companies: Consider hiring the services of a professional penetration testing or cybersecurity company. They have expertise in analyzing and mitigating malware threats and can provide tailored assistance for your specific project.

Remember, analyzing malware can be complex and time-consuming. It is important to ensure you have the necessary knowledge and take proper precautions to avoid any potential harm to your systems or data.
